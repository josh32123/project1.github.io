Unveiling Growth Patterns: Data Analysis and Prediction for Electric Vehicle Charging Stations

Analyzing electric vehicle (EV) charging station data is vital due to increasing electricity demand, reduced oil dependency, and lower carbon emissions. This study reviewed data from 2011 to 2020, examining transaction fees, charging levels, and session-end methods. Data visualization revealed annual trends in charging times, energy use, greenhouse gas (GHG) reductions, and gasoline savings. A notable decline in energy consumption in 2020 was attributed to the COVID-19 pandemic. The SKTime algorithm forecasts metrics from 2021 to 2026, anticipating growth in average charging time, energy use, GHG reductions, and gasoline savings. The results underscore the influence of EV technology on energy consumption and sustainability, highlighting the need for robust EV charging infrastructure to mitigate oil reliance and emissions.
